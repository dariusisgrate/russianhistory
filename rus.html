Kievan Rus' (862–1240)

Overview

The Kievan Rus' marks the foundation of Russian statehood, emerging in 862 when the Varangian prince Rurik established rule in Novgorod, uniting Slavic tribes under a single banner.

Key Events

This period saw the rise of key cities like Kiev, which became the political and cultural center under leaders like Oleg and Vladimir the Great. The adoption of Christianity in 988 by Vladimir I was a pivotal moment, aligning the Rus' with the Byzantine Empire and shaping its religious and cultural identity.

The Kievan Rus' was a loose federation of principalities, thriving through trade along the Dnieper River and the "Route from the Varangians to the Greeks." However, internal conflicts and external pressures, such as the Mongol invasion in the 13th century, led to its decline by 1240.
