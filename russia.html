Modern Russia

A Journey of Resilience and Renewal Since 1991

Key Historical Milestones

Dissolution of the USSR (1991)

The USSR dissolved on December 31, 1991, following the Belavezha Accords. Russia emerged as an independent state under President Boris Yeltsin, marking the beginning of a turbulent transition to a market economy.

Economic Reforms (1990s)

The 1990s saw "shock therapy" reforms, privatizing state enterprises. While this created a new class of oligarchs, it also led to economic hardship, with GDP dropping by nearly 40% and hyperinflation peaking at 2,500% in 1992.

Putin's Era (2000–Present)

Vladimir Putin became president in 2000, stabilizing the economy through oil exports and centralizing power. His leadership saw GDP growth averaging 7% annually in the 2000s, though it also raised concerns over democratic freedoms.

Crimea Annexation (2014)

Russia annexed Crimea in 2014 following a controversial referendum, leading to international sanctions and heightened tensions with the West, particularly the United States and NATO.

Cultural Highlights

Modern Russian culture is a vibrant blend of tradition and innovation. Literature, music, and ballet remain world-renowned, with figures like author Dmitry Glukhovsky and the Bolshoi Ballet gaining global acclaim. The Russian Orthodox Church has seen a revival, influencing societal values.

Digital culture has also flourished, with platforms like VKontakte (Russia's equivalent to Facebook) and a booming gaming industry. Annual events like Maslenitsa and Victory Day celebrations showcase Russia's rich traditions and national pride.

Economic Overview

Russia's economy is heavily reliant on natural resources, particularly oil and gas, which account for about 60% of exports as of 2025. The country has the world's 11th-largest GDP, valued at approximately $1.8 trillion USD in 2024, according to the World Bank. However, sanctions since 2014 have pushed economic diversification, with growth in tech sectors like cybersecurity (e.g., Kaspersky Lab).

Despite progress, challenges remain: income inequality is high, with the top 10% owning 46% of wealth, and inflation has averaged 5–6% annually in recent years, impacting living standards.

Global Role

Russia remains a major global player, holding a permanent seat on the UN Security Council. Its military spending, around $65 billion in 2024, supports a formidable presence, including nuclear capabilities. Russia has expanded its influence in regions like the Middle East (e.g., Syria) and Africa through arms deals and energy partnerships.

However, geopolitical tensions persist, particularly with the West over Ukraine and cybersecurity issues. Russia's pivot to Asia, especially its partnership with China, has strengthened, with bilateral trade reaching $240 billion in 2024.
